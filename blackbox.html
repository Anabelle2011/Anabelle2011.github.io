Black Box Investigation

Introduction

In the beginning of the school year Mr. Sinusas challenged our class to infer what was inside his mystery black box. He showed us a demonstration where he poured the same amount of water (in ml) several times in a row into the funnel of the box. Since the body of the box is completely black nothing inside was visible to our class. Each time the water was poured into the box a different amount of water came out of the bottom. Essentially our job was to infer what was happening inside the box based on background knowledge we may have had and knowledge that we retained during the demonstration.

Claim

Our group developed the idea that there was a system of pipes in the box where some pipes lead to storage containers that will eventually overflow and some lead straight to the exit.

Evidence And Reasoning

Mr. Sinusas demonstrated his model by pouring in 1000ml of water into the funnel two times in a row and when 0ml of water came out we knew that whatever was inside the box must have been able to hold 2000ml. A total of 47 different trials took place, all of them using different variations of water. We designed our box with the idea in mind that there must be storage. Our design included 3 pipes coming straight from the funnel. The pipes on the left and right both led to storage. Once the water in the storage containers reached a certain point a hole would lead the water out by pipe and to the exit. If we go back to the funnel the small middle pipe in between the furthest left and right pipes would take the water down right to the exit. The idea originally was that if the water was poured faster into the funnel some of the water would skim over the surface of the small middle pipe because of how small it was. If the water did skim over we expected it to lead down the left and right pipes into storage. If the water was poured into the funnel slower we expected it to go right down the middle pipe and to the exit straight away. For our first trial we poured 100 ml into the box slowly. We got 50 ml as our output with a note that some water was spilled. For our second trial we poured in 100ml fast and got 60 ml out with a note that water spilled. Eventually we tested all variations of water being poured (slow, fast, more water, less water). Our design had many leaks that affected many of our final results. For much of the experiment less water consistently came out then was poured in due to the many leaks. After we patched as many leaks as possible the amount of water that came out started to vary as some water was getting stored. We poured 80ml of water into the box two separate times (one being faster, one being slower). During the faster test only 45ml came out of the bottom while the slower test had 50ml coming out the bottom. This proved that some of the water went to storage during the faster test because it skimmed over the smaller middle hole and went into the tubes leading to storage. This is why less water came out the bottom during the faster pour. Overall the design had flaws that affected the results but once the flaws were fixed (for the most part) the results we were getting started to match the ones of the original black box design. Models are a visual tool that can help the creators imagine their thoughts in front of them. They can be helpful to see your ideas but they may not always be as accurate as the original designs. It was helpful to visualize our idea in front of us but since it was built with flaws (leaks and wrong placement of some items) it was hard to determine if it was truly similar to the original design. It will always be simpler to represent models rather than the real thing because for a model you can focus on one aspect of something. This can help us expand our ideas in one topic which helps make the whole process easier.My claim that there is a system of pipes and storage inside the black box can be supported by the fact that a different amount of water comes out of the box each time water is poured in. Our box (after some leaks were patched) supported our claim because some water did end up getting stored inside while some water ended up flowing out the bottom as expected. On the other hand, if there was a water tipping bucket in place of the tubes and storage we would observe that every few trials a large amount of water would come out of the bottom and in between those trials few to no water would come out. These observations would not match those of the original black box meaning that a water tipping bucket is not what is inside the box.

Photography with professional dSLR cameras

As we took photos, we thought about many things including the rule of thirds, framing and angle to take our pictures. We learned about the rule of thirds first. Essentially the rule of thirds utilizes the guidelines on a camera that allow us to control how our photo looks by placing our subject or what we are capturing in a certain position. We utilized the rule of thirds in our project by drawing more attention to certain things in our picture. For example when pouring water into the funnel we chose to focus on the cylinder rather than the person pouring the water. This helps make photos look more professional.

Next we learned about framing which can help add depth to our photo by controlling how the subject is peered upon. Framing helps draw attention to the subject or what is being captured. We utilized framing during this project by using the boxs actual frame to draw attention to the inside of the box. This helps direct the viewers attention to its intended target.

After this we learned about angles which help control how much power a photo has. By angling the camera in a certain way we can tell a story. For example a lower angled camera shot can give the subject more power because it seems as though they are bigger than us. With this in mind we were able to capture photos of our box with the intention of the viewer having more or less power to make the box seem insignificant and significant at the same time. This helped to add a story to our photos.

2D design and animation in adobe photoshop

Before we started to build or model we were assigned with the task of creating a 2d digital model on adobe photoshop. Adobe photoshop is a useful tool that many creators use for many different purposes. We used adobe to help brainstorm ideas then create them digitally as for what we thought was inside the black box. We learned how to create more interesting models by using different brushes and tools. After this we learned how to animate our models which helped everyone visualize how their models would perform. The animation aspect of the project used frame by frame methods where every frame something small would have to change in order to create the story. By using adobe photoshop we could easily visualize our ideas and have our models come to life.

3D design and animation in Autodesk Maya

After we tested our physical model we were assigned to create a 3D version of our black box using the modeling tool that many Disney movies were created on. Autodesk Maya allowed us to create much more realistic models of our black box. First, we learned how to move around objects by using the controls such as translate or scale. Next, we learned how to add more realism to our boxes by using extrude (which adds thickness to an object), bevel (which adds dimensions to an object) and many more tools that helped to manipulate the objects. Then, we customized the material (which affects how the object looks such as the color or texture). Also, we customized the lighting to improve the rendering of our models. After that, we learned how to rig a hand in Maya which controlled how the joints of the hand moved. To rig you must add points onto the object and then mesh together the point and the object to control its movement. This added more realism to the model. We then learned how to utilize the camera by key framing and adding cuts to our animation. This simulated a real movie or show and how they use cameras to add depth to a scene. We would position the camera as we wanted then we could cut or keyframe. The final thing we learned how to do in Maya was add a water simulation. This was a very complex process that included connecting many components together behind the scenes which resulted in a very realistic water effect on the scene. All of these components helped to create an interesting and realistic scene of our black box model.
