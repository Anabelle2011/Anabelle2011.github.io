Screenprinting Project

Screenprinting Process

In the beginning of quarter three, Mr. Sinusas introduced us to the screenprint project. He showed us last year's project and started to explain how we would replicate it. One of the first non-physical steps was to think of any cause we cared about. This could range between making people smile with a joke to climate change affecting our world. After doing research and analyzing examples, everyone chose what they would choose to be working on for the next quarter. Personally, I chose to focus on political issues that have been affecting our country. Although political views are controversial I felt that it may be important to share my opinion on it. I grew up in a very blue leaning household so I tend to agree with many democratic ideas. Since president Trump became our nation's leader again, I have felt that the world has been suffering negatively. There are many issues that I care about relating to this political case but I chose to focus on the general issue of the government's downfall. We generated design ideas on photoshop and we learned many features to help convey our message. The ultimate goal of the project would be to convince peers or any random person to think about our cause and take action. Since the goal was to convince people of something we had to factor that into our project. I knew that the print should be eye-catching so I chose very bright blue and red colors to both symbolize the political parties and attract attention. As we developed our design we were instructed to add symbolic text that relates to our cause. I chose to add the famous quote “united we stand divided we fall” because I want to both inform people of the issue and tell them what they can do to help. In the end, I chose to convey my message through a large red fist about to crush the white house and many protestors shown as blue. I think that everyone needs to work together to run the country and if enough people are convinced, then it may be able to happen. After digital designing, we moved towards physical designing. We were provided with wood boards and a guideline to make a frame. Everyone's frames ranged in size depending on what they wanted to print. Mine was on the smaller side based on my personal preference. We attached the screen onto the frame and soon after we actually did the printing. The printing was a step by step process of lining up the frame to whatever we were printing on, then very carefully spreading the ink across the screen. Once we finished one print we would move on to the next until we had paper, fabric and a box that would be used later. After the project was complete the class took turns on working on a digital video explanation of the project and sewing the fabrics together. The videos were very important because they summed up everything we had learned. It took hard work to edit well but in the end, the videos turned out well and it proved that we had learned a lot of new things. The sewing is very symbolic because the stitches tie everyone's causes together much like how the real world ties causes with others. In the end, everyone's causes tell a story together that will hopefully be able to convince some people to take action.

21st Century Skill

Throughout the project, I think I have most developed my problem solving skills. After reflecting on everything, most causes that people chose were actually real problems in the world. Everyone had to essentially convey the problem and think of ways to solve it at the same time. The print is supposed to be informational but also motivational. This was difficult to achieve because it's hard to tell two stories through the same thing. I thought about how we would be able to solve the issue and that is how I was led to add the quote "united we stand divided we fall” . The quote conveys the message that if we are all divided everything turns to chaos but if we work together we can solve things in a more effective way. After this process, I have learned to use my problem solving skills in the real world when political topics are brought up.

Physical Procedure

  • Measure wooden blocks to the desired size
  • Make cut list
  • Cut each block according to the cut list
  • Glue the frame together
